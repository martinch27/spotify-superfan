05 · Asked everywhere

Why this, why now.

How is this different from Patreon? +
Two things. First, the audience is already here — SuperFan lives inside Spotify, surfaced to the fans who already listen to you. You don't need to build a separate funnel; the fans are one tap away from the track they're playing right now. Second, your content is actually protected: SuperFan ships with watermarking, screenshot detection, automated DMCA, and GDPR right-to-erasure on day one. Pre-release snippets, exclusive drops, and behind-the-scenes content stay where you put them — not leaked across the internet within hours.
Does this kill my streaming revenue? +
No. Streaming royalties continue exactly as today — Premium and Free tier listening, Discovery Mode, playlist pitching, Marquee, all untouched. SuperFan is an additive revenue layer on top of streaming, with a different mechanic (recurring subscriptions vs per-stream micropayments).
Why a 20% take rate? +
Because we run the whole machine for you. Payment processing across 180+ markets, the content-protection stack (watermarking, screenshot detection, automated DMCA, GDPR compliance), customer support, platform reliability, and ongoing product development — all on us. More importantly, you're not paying to acquire your audience: they're already on Spotify, listening to you today. We bring qualified, engaged listeners directly into your SuperFan tier with zero outbound effort from you.
What happens to my merch and concert revenue? +
Untouched. Spotify takes 0% on merch (via Shopify integration, as today) and 0% on tickets (via Ticketmaster/AXS/Eventim affiliate, as today). The new option: you can mark merch drops as SuperFan-exclusive, or offer presale codes through your promoter. You decide.
What is "the pilot" exactly? +
1,000 independent artists, 90-day program, $10K Year-1 revenue floor (Spotify covers any shortfall). Selection prioritizes the DIY mid-tier where unit economics are clearest. After the pilot: open access for every eligible independent artist.
